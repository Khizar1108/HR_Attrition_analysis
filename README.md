# 📊 HR Attrition Analysis (Excel + SQL + Power BI)

## 🚀 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition using a combination of Excel, SQL, and Power BI.  
The goal is to understand why employees leave and identify key patterns that can help organizations improve retention.

Instead of just building visuals, this project covers the complete data analysis workflow — from data cleaning to insights.

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
- Excel → Data cleaning & preprocessing  
- SQL → Data analysis & querying insights  
- Power BI → Dashboard & data visualization  

## 📂 Dataset

**Dataset link:**[HR_ATTRITION_ANALYSIS_DATASET](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/pavansubhasht/ibm-hr-analytics-attrition-dataset)

- HR Employee Attrition Dataset  
- ~1470 employee records  
- Includes:
  - Age, Gender, Department  
  - Job Role, Job Satisfaction  
  - Monthly Income, Salary Hike %  
  - Years at Company  
  - Attrition (Yes/No)  

## 🔍 Project Workflow

### 1️⃣ Data Cleaning (Excel)
- Removed duplicates  
- Checked missing values  
- Standardized column formats  

### 2️⃣ Data Analysis (SQL)
- Analyzed attrition by department  
- Identified salary impact on attrition  
- Checked experience vs attrition trends  
- Found high-risk employee segments  

### 3️⃣ Data Visualization (Power BI)
Built an interactive dashboard with:

#### 🔹 Executive Overview
- Total Employees, Attrition Count, Attrition Rate  
- Attrition by Department & Job Role  
- Attrition by Salary Group & Age Group  

#### 🔹 Demographics Analysis
- Attrition by Gender  
- Attrition by Age Group  
- Attrition by Education & Marital Status  

#### 🔹 Job & Experience Analysis
- Attrition by Years at Company  
- Attrition by Job Level  
- Attrition by Job Satisfaction  

#### 🔹 Department Analysis
- Attrition by Department (detailed)  
- Environment Satisfaction impact  
- Business Travel impact  

#### 🔹 Salary Analysis
- Salary Distribution (Histogram)  
- Attrition by Salary Group  
- Attrition by Salary Hike %  

## 📈 Key Insights
- Employees with low salary have higher attrition  
- R&D and Sales departments show more exits  
- Employees in early career stages leave more  
- Low job satisfaction is a major factor  
- Frequent business travel increases attrition  

## 💡 Conclusion
This project demonstrates how combining Excel, SQL, and Power BI can provide meaningful business insights.  
The dashboard can help HR teams take better decisions to reduce employee attrition.
